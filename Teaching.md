<p align="justify">Through formal training combined with research and teaching experience, I have developed expertise that makes me well suited to teach a number of topics across the cognitive, social, and computational sciences including but not limited to: human cognition; learning and instruction; human-computer interaction; data and information visualization; data analytics; modeling and simulation; complex adaptive systems; digital ethics; epistemology; research methods; and psychometrics.</p>

<p align="justify">I have experience teaching graduate students at the University of Central Florida as a lecturer in the Cognitive Sciences Program and a graduate teaching assistant in the Modeling and Simulation program. I have also contributed to the assessment of the institutional effectiveness of graduate programs at UCF, using both quantitative measures of performance and qualitative masures of student perceptions of learning to evaluate instructional approoaches. </p>
