<p align="justify">
To date, I have conducted research in the areas of instructional systems and artificial social intelligence to support teamwork, social behavior in online platforms, social cues and social signals in human-robot interaction, cognition and collaboration in space missions, the science of team science, and diversity in open source software development. My research has been accepted and published in journals, conferences, and workshops associated with leading organizations including <a href="https://www.ieee.org/">IEEE</a>, <a href="https://www.acm.org/">ACM</a>, <a href="https://www.informs.org/">INFORMS</a>, and <a href="https://www.hfes.org/">HFES</a>.</p> 
<p align="justify">
I was a McKnight Doctoral Fellow at the University of Central Florida where I earned a Ph.D. conferred by the College of Engineering and Computer Science. I previously earned a M.S. in Modeling and Simulation and a graduate certificate in Cognitive Science. My dissertation research examining the relationship beween diversity and corporatization in open source software projects was initially developed at the <a href="https://www.santafe.edu/">Santa Fe Institute</a> and refined with guidance from an interdisciplinary group of mentors: <a href="https://csl.ist.ucf.edu/People">Stephen M. Fiore</a>, <a href="https://www.eecs.ucf.edu/~gitars/">Gita Sukthankar</a>, <a href="https://kblincoe.github.io/">Kelly Blincoe</a>, <a href="https://www.cs.ucf.edu/~garibay/">Ivan Garibay</a>, and <a href="https://business.ucf.edu/person/carol-saunders/">Carol Saunders</a>. I received the 2022-23 University Award for <a href="https://www.ist.ucf.edu/dr-olivia-newton-ph-d-university-of-central-florida-2022-2023-outstanding-dissertation/"><i>Outstanding Dissertation</i></a> from UCF which recognizes the quality, content, and exceptional contribution of my research.</p>
